Frequently Asked Questions

Honest answers about importing a car from Japan to Kenya.

What is QISJ and what does the certificate prove?

QISJ (Quality Inspection Standard Japan) is an independent third-party inspector that examines every car before it leaves Japan. The certificate documents the car’s actual condition, verified mileage (so it can’t be tampered with later), and any defects. You receive your QISJ certificate together with the bill of lading.

What is JUMVEA and can I see your exporter’s certificate before paying?

JUMVEA (Japan Used Motor Vehicle Exporters Association) is the official trade body that vets and registers used car exporters in Japan. Membership requires a verifiable business address, a registered license, and ongoing code-of-conduct compliance. We work with major JUMVEA-certified exporters like BE FORWARD and GOONET Exchange — and yes, the JUMVEA certificate is shared with you before any payment. Just ask our Sales Team on WhatsApp.

Is duty and tax (VAT) included in the price?

The displayed price is FOB Japan (port-to-port). Kenya import duty, VAT, IDF and clearance are not included. Use the Total Landed Cost calculator on any car page for a realistic estimate to Mombasa or Nairobi.

How long does shipping from Japan to Mombasa take?

RoRo shipping takes approximately 4–6 weeks from Japan to Mombasa Port. Container Share is similar but slightly faster on average and offers more protection for high-value vehicles.

Can you handle the clearance at Mombasa Port?

Yes. We work with bonded clearing agents at Mombasa Port and can deliver the car directly to your location in Nairobi, Kisumu, Eldoret or anywhere in Kenya.

Do you accept M-Pesa payments?

Yes. The USD 2,000 deposit can be paid via M-Pesa (KES equivalent) or direct bank transfer. The remaining CIF balance is typically paid via bank transfer (Wise, SWIFT) for security and tax-record purposes.

What documents do I need to import a car to Kenya?

We provide the full export package: Japanese export certificate, QISJ pre-shipment inspection, bill of lading, commercial invoice and KEBS conformity certificate. You only need your KRA PIN and ID for clearance.
